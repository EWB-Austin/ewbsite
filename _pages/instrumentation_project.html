Projects: 1) Body Temperature Incubator (Completed Revision 1, Revision 2 in Progress) 2) Soil Moisture Sensor (Competed Revision 1)

Summary: Almost all EWB-USA projects can benefit from instrumentation systems in terms of Digitized Measurement Tools, Automation & Control Systems, Power Generation Systems, Wireless Gadgets and many others. Unfortunately, many times these systems or gadgets are cost prohibitive for EWB-USA projects due to high initial cost and recurring maintenance costs. The technical expertise, knowledge base and learning curve can also be difficult to attain for the project leads and the local community due to limited access to resources and closed/intellectually-protected systems. Considering these challenges, the Instrumentation group’s aim is to provide the most cost effective and best possible solution to strategically increase work efficiency, accuracy, productivity and the overall outcome of the projects.

We utilize open-source electronics and hardware as much as possible for easy accessibility, fast learning curve and surprisingly low cost. Here are some of the resources we have utilized to make the Soil Moisture Sensor and Body Temperature Incubator.

http://www.arduino.cc
http://reprap.org

We’re open to accepting instrumentation projects from other Non-profit organizations.

We can utilize the skills of Mechanical Engineers, Electrical Engineers, Computer Engineers, Scientists, Makers and Hackers.
